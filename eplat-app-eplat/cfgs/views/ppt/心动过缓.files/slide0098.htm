SUGGESTIONS TO INSTRUCTORS
Keep repeating how simple the treatment of symptomatic bradycardias is.
Treatment summary
1. If there are serious signs or symptoms due to the bradycardia: treat the    bradycardia.
2. Treat using this intervention sequence:
     atropine
     transcutaneous pacing
     dopamine
     epinephrine
     isoproterenol
3. If there are NOT serious signs and symptoms, ask whether the    bradycardia fits the criteria for one of the “red-flag” bradycardias:
   Type II second-degree AV block or third-degree AV block.
4. If a “red-flag” bradycardia is present: prepare for transvenous pacing    while using transcutaneous pacing.
Atropine is for temporary stabilization only.
Dopamine infusions are useful for patients who do not respond to atropine and those for whom TCP is delayed or impossible due to non-capture, unavailability, or malfunction.
