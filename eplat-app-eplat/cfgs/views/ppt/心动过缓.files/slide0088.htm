Identify A, B, and C
A = Sinus bradycardia
Rate = 48 bpm; regular.
B = Third-degree AV block at the supra-nodal level
The atrial rate = 45-70 bpm; ventricular rate = 44 bpm. The narrow QRS indicates that the block is occurring above the ventricles (supraventricular) at the level of the AV node.
C = Third-degree AV block at the infra-nodal (bundle branch) level
The wide QRS indicates that the block is occurring at the ventricular level. There is no relation between the atrial and ventricular rhythm. Ventricular rhythm is regular and very slow (38 bpm). The QRS is wide because block is at the bundle branch level, usually involving both bundle branches.
Which is most likely her rhythm?
She experienced no chest pain or ischemic symptoms prior to the onset of her weakness and shortness of breath. Therefore, the only rhythm not associated with acute pain, rhythm A, is the more likely cause of her symptomatic bradycardia. Rhythm A is due to increased para- sympathetic tone or medication side effects. Rhythm B is unlikely because it is often seen after acute ischemia, though it can occur from toxic drug effects. Rhythm C is almost always caused by an occlusion of the left anterior descending artery. AMIs associated with that artery are large, serious, and painful.
