Confirm that the “flat line” is not a result of operator error, which is a
more common cause of a flat ECG than fine VF. (Note: One good clue to
the possibility of operator error is when “asystolic” patients have a pulse.)
Be sure to check the following:
•Ensure proper connection of the monitor cable to the patient.
•Verify proper connection of the monitor cable to the defibrillator.
•Ensure that the power switch is ON.
Because the lists of possible reversible causes of asystolic and PEA
cardiac arrest are the same, you can use the same memory aids. Below
is the memory aid “the 5 H’s and the 5 T’s Approach”:
Five Reversible Causes That Start With H
•Hypoxia (CNS events)
•Hypokalemia/hyperkalemia (and other electrolytes)
•Hypothermia/hyperthermia
•Hypoglycemia/hyperglycemia
•Hypovolemia (tank/anaphylaxis, gravid)
Five Reversible Causes That Start With T
•Trauma
•Tamponade
•Thrombosis (pulmonary) / Thrombosis (coronary)
•Tablets (OD, drugs, etc)
•Tension (pneumothorax, asthma)
