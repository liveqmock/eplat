Note time in relation to the 3-hour window for fibrinolytic therapy.
Answer 1: In the first hours after an ischemic stroke the CT scan is usually normal. After 3 to 12 hours the ischemic or infarcted area of the stroke swells with edema and evolves into an area of hypodensity. A well-defined hypodensity indicating blocked blood flow is rarely observed within the first 3 hours of a stroke. Patients with well-defined hypodensities should not be treated with fibrinolytics.
Answer 2: If course participants are unfamiliar with evaluating CT scans, instructors should point out that hypodense areas indicate lack of blood flow as well as excessive fluid (CSF) and edema; hyperdense areas indicate blood, which is slightly more dense than brain tissue. Acute subarachnoid bleeding spreads rapidly throughout the subarachnoid space, producing hyperdense (white) areas on the CT scan throughout much of the subarachnoid space.
