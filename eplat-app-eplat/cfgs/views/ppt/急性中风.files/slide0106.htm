寻找其他的不正常表现
Facial droop: Have the patient show teeth and smile; note facial motor function. Look for asymmetry:
   Normal: Both sides of face move equally or not at all.
   Abnormal: One side of patient’s face droops.
CMouth does not turn up on right side.
CNasolabial fold is flatter on right side.
CPalpebral fissure is wider on right side.
CNote that forehead wrinkles are symmetrical since they are not affected by stroke.
Motor weakness: Have the patient close eyes and extend arms, palms down.
   Normal: Both arms remain extended equally, drift down equally, or    do not move.
   Abnormal: One arm drifts down when compared with the other.
Speech: Ask the patient to repeat a phrase.
   Normal: Phrase is repeated clearly and correctly.
   Abnormal: Words are slurred (dysarthria) or abnormal (aphasia), or    patient is mute.
Note: This scale can still be used if the patient is unconscious. Provide a central painful stimulus (rub the sternum or pinch the trapezius) to evaluate symmetry of grimace; pinch the medial aspect of each extremity to evaluate symmetry of abduction from painful stimulus.
