Facial droop: Ask the patient to show his or her teeth and to smile; note facial motor function. Look for asymmetry:
   Normal: Both sides of face move equally well or not at all.
   Abnormal: One side of patient’s face droops.
These figures illustrate a right facial droop consistent with left hemispheric stroke. Note: Asymmetry is not as obvious when the face is relaxed but becomes more apparent when the patient is asked to smile and show teeth. Ask course participants to point out the diagnostic asymmetrical features. They should note
 1. Mouth does not turn up on right side.
 2. Nasolabial fold is flatter on right side.
 3. Palpebral fissure is wider on right side.
 4. Note that the forehead does wrinkle because of cross-over    innervating. Suspect Bell’s palsy if the forehead muscles appear
   weak or paralyzed.
