Treatment should include
•Close monitoring and control of blood pressure
•Continuous ECG monitoring because arrhythmias can occur after stroke
•Assessment and support of cardiorespiratory function, including use of pulse oximetry and oxygen if indicated
•Evaluation and treatment of blood glucose if needed
•Administration of thiamine (100 mg) if the patient is malnourished or alcoholic
•Control of fever with acetaminophen
•Do not administer aspirin or heparin for 24 hours after administration of fibrinolytics (although previous aspirin administration is not a contraindication to fibrinolytics).
•Keep patient NPO.
•Monitor neurologic status closely.
•IV: avoid hypotonic (D5W) fluid administration and routine administration of dextrose-containing solutions. Glucose is required if hypoglycemia is documented, however.
•Assess for seizures and treat if needed.
•Control intracranial pressure if necessary.
