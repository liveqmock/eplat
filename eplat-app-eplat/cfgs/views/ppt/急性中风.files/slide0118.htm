Answer 1: Management of blood pressure in stroke patients is controversial. Few data exist even to provide consensus recommendations. The guidelines provided in the ACLS Textbook are tailored to stroke type (ischemic versus hemorrhagic) and vary if thrombolytic therapy is provided. In general, emergent antihypertensive therapy is unnecessary for most stroke patients. However, aggressive control of blood pressure is provided if hemorrhagic stroke is present or thrombolytic therapy has been administered.
Answer 2: It may be helpful to point out the 3 major groups of stroke patients who require firm control of hypertension:
CCandidates for fibrinolytics
CPatients receiving fibrinolytics
CPatients with hemorrhagic stroke
