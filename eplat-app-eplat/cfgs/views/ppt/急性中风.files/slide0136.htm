Answer: Fibrinolytic therapy is not an option for this patient, who has suffered an intracerebral hemorrhage (hemorrhagic stroke).
CAccording to the 2000 ECC Handbook, emergency antihypertensive therapy calls for treatment with IV labetalol bolus (10 mg), possibly followed by an infusion.
CSodium nitroprusside may be used for severe hypertension. Nitroprusside has the advantage of immediate onset and short duration of action. This drug should be considered if the patient’s blood pressure rises further or fails to respond to labetalol.
CIf the patient has contraindications to -blockers, consider sodium nitroprusside, enalapril, or nicardipine.
Note: Data is extremely limited about blood pressure ranges requiring therapy for patients with hemorrhagic stroke, so the instructor may wish to ask about course participants’ experience and protocols at their institutions. Blood pressure should be monitored closely to avoid relative hypotension.
