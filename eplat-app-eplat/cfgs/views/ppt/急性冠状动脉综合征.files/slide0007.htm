The history in this first case should be “classic,” with all elements of the textbook acute heart attack. Later cases can be used to point out the large variety of ways in which acute coronary syndromes present.

ACLS provider trainees should be taught to apply the 1-to-10 pain scale commonly used in prehospital care.

Trainees should be taught the importance of recording the time of pain onset. This is relevant to later decisions about thrombolytic therapy and primary PTCA.
