•All ACLS providers should have access to the new ACLS educational
•materials:
•2000 ECC Handbook
•ECC Guidelines 2000
•ACLS Provider Manual (2001)
•ACLS Textbook (2001)
•Participants in the ACLS Provider Course should refer to these sources of
•information during case discussions.
•The 2000 ECC Handbook provides easy reference to details of all recommended medications, all the AHA algorithms, and an excellent 24-page section on acute coronary syndromes prepared by John Field, MD.
•The history in this first case should be “classic,” with all elements of the textbook acute heart attack. Later cases can be used to point out the large variety of ways in which acute coronary syndromes present.
• 
•ACLS provider trainees should be taught to apply the 1-to-10 pain scale commonly used in prehospital care.
• 
•Trainees should be taught the importance of recording the time of pain onset. This is relevant to later decisions about thrombolytic therapy and primary PTCA.

•Note that not all of these approaches are used in every community. Blood draws for laboratory tests, for example, may not be indicated in all EMS systems. •EMT-Ps can begin gathering information related to inclusion/exclusion criteria for fibrinolytic therapy. •EMT-P units can use 12-lead ECGs with computerized analyses to alert the ED that an AMI patient is en route to the hospital. Studies have shown that the “alerting phenomenon” can dramatically decrease the time from call for help to administration of fibrinolytic therapy. •Not all of these assessments are necessary or available in the pre-hospital setting.

•This slide illustrates the 3 ´ 4 format of the 12-lead ECG.
•Each box represents 1 lead, and the viewpoint of that lead is indicated.
•
•Review the 12-lead ECG.
•Go lead by lead, and pick one good complex in each lead.
•Find the J point and ST segment.
•Compare the ST to the TP segment, looking for 1 mm (1 small box) of elevation (ignore ST depression for now).
•Place a check mark next to any lead with 1 mm of ST-segment elevation.
•Localize the area of infarction.
